Nalbari District

Nalbari district, located in western Assam, is known for its cultural richness, fertile plains, and historical significance. Carved out of Kamrup district in 1985, it has Nalbari town as its administrative headquarters. The district thrives on agriculture, with paddy, mustard, and jute being the primary crops. Home to educational institutions and religious sites like Hari Mandir and Shripur Dewalaya, Nalbari blends tradition with gradual modernization, making it a vibrant part of Assam.
